# 📊 Key Insights – IPL Player Performance Analysis

## 🔍 Batting Insights

- Lower middle-order batters appear to have lower total runs primarily due to limited opportunities (15–30 balls per match), rather than lack of ability

- A small group of top-order players contributes the majority of total runs, highlighting their importance in team stability and match outcomes

- While established players like V. Kohli and AB de Villiers dominate performance metrics, emerging domestic players such as M.K. Pandey, S.S. Iyer, S.A. Yadav, and H.H. Pandya demonstrate strong recent form and offer high potential at a lower acquisition cost

---

## 🎯 Bowling Insights

- Extras conceded by bowlers are highly influenced by match situations rather than inefficiency alone

- Bowlers operating in high-pressure phases (e.g., death overs) tend to concede more extras due to aggressive gameplay and risk-taking

- Death-over specialists (e.g., Malinga) show higher extras due to extreme pressure scenarios, making raw statistics insufficient for evaluation

- New-ball bowlers (e.g., P. Kumar, B. Kumar) often concede extras while attempting swing, indicating a trade-off between aggression and control

---

## 💼 Strategic Insights

- Performance evaluation should incorporate match context rather than relying solely on aggregate statistics

- Investing in emerging domestic players can provide strong performance returns while optimizing auction budgets

- Experienced bowlers featured in high-impact roles remain valuable despite higher extras, due to their strategic importance in crucial match situations
